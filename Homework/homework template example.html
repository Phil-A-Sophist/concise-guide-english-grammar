Part 5: Revision and Reflection (approx. 10–15 minutes)

Instructions: This exercise asks you to apply what you've learned to improve a paragraph of writing.
  1. Read the original paragraph below.
  2. Rewrite the entire paragraph, making improvements based on concepts from this unit. You might combine sentences, add detail, fix errors, or restructure for clarity.
  3. Reflect on your changes: In 3–5 sentences, explain what you changed and why. Reference specific grammatical concepts from this unit.
✓ Example (completed):

Original paragraph:

The dog ran. The dog was fast. The dog chased a squirrel. The squirrel climbed a tree. The dog barked.

Revised paragraph:

The fast dog ran across the yard, chasing a squirrel that quickly climbed the nearest oak tree. Frustrated, the dog barked at the base of the trunk.

Reflection:

I combined the choppy sentences using coordination and subordination. I turned "the dog was fast" into an adjective ("fast dog") and added a relative clause ("that quickly climbed the nearest oak tree") to combine information about the squirrel. I also added a participial phrase ("Frustrated") to show the dog's emotion while connecting the final action. These changes improve flow and reduce repetition.
